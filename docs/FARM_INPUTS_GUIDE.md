# Farm Inputs Recommendation Guide

## What is This?

This is a smart system that tells you exactly what farm products (fertilizers, pesticides, seeds, tools) you need to grow the crops recommended by Fahamu Shamba.

It helps you:
✓ Know exactly what to buy
✓ Know how much to buy
✓ Know when to apply it
✓ Know how much it will cost
✓ Find ways to save money
✓ Improve your soil before planting

---

## How It Works

### Step 1: Get Crop Recommendations
First, use Fahamu Shamba to get crop recommendations based on your farm conditions:
- Your location (sub-county)
- Your soil type
- Current season
- Your budget
- Your farm size

### Step 2: Get Input Requirements
For each recommended crop, get a detailed list of:
- **Fertilizers** - What nutrients the crop needs
- **Pesticides** - What pests and diseases to prevent
- **Seeds/Planting Material** - Quality seeds to use
- **Tools** - Equipment you'll need
- **Soil Amendments** - How to prepare soil

### Step 3: Adjust to Your Budget
Get the input recommendations prioritized by importance:
- **Essential** - Must buy for good yield
- **Important** - Strongly recommended
- **Optional** - Nice to have if budget allows

### Step 4: Get Cost-Saving Tips
Learn practical ways to reduce costs while keeping productivity high.

## Cost-Saving Strategies

### 1. Group Buying
**Save: 15-25%**
- Join farmer group or cooperative
- Buy inputs together in bulk
- Get better prices
- **Contact:** Visit sub-county agricultural office

### 2. Use Farmyard Manure
**Save: 30-40%**
- Use animal manure instead of all fertilizers
- Requires: 10-15 tons/ha available
- Better for soil health long-term
- **How:** Collect from animals, compost, apply before planting

### 3. Organic Pest Control
**Save: 25-35%**
- Neem oil spray (from neem leaves)
- Soap spray (for soft-bodied insects)
- Companion planting (plant together strategically)
- **Advantage:** Safer for family and environment

### 4. Mulching
**Save: 20-30%**
- Use farm waste: maize stalks, grass, leaves
- Reduces watering needs
- Reduces weeding needs
- Improves soil

### 5. Seed Saving
**Save: 15-25%**
- Save best seeds from your harvest
- **Best for:** Open-pollinated varieties (not hybrids)
- **Advantage:** Seeds adapted to your farm
- **Note:** Hybrid seeds won't breed true

### 6. Crop Rotation
**Save: 15-20%**
- Follow legumes (beans, groundnuts) with cereals
- Legumes fix nitrogen naturally
- Reduces fertilizer needs next season

## Soil Improvement: What to Do BEFORE Planting

### If Your Soil is Sandy
**Issues:** Low fertility, poor water retention

**What to add:**
- Farmyard Manure (10-15 tons/ha)
- Compost (8-12 tons/ha)
- Cost: 5,000-7,500 KSh/ha
- Timing: 2-3 months before planting

### If Your Soil is Acidic (pH too low)
**Issues:** Nutrients locked up, poor growth

**What to add:**
- Lime/CaCO3 (2-3 tons/ha)
- Cost: 2,400-3,600 KSh/ha
- Timing: 3-6 months before planting

### If Soil Quality is Good
**Just maintain it:**
- Add manure every season
- Rotate crops yearly
- Keep soil covered (mulch)
- Prevent erosion

## Safety Guidelines

### Chemical Safety
**ALWAYS:**
- Wear gloves, mask, hat when handling chemicals
- Spray early morning or late evening (not midday)
- Keep away from children and animals
- Store in cool, dark, dry place
- Use original containers
- Keep chemicals away from food and water

**NEVER:**
- Don't spray on windy days
- Don't eat/drink while handling chemicals
- Don't exceed recommended doses
- Don't spray near water sources
- Don't spray within harvest time (read label)

### After Spraying
- Wash hands and face
- Change clothes
- Wash sprayer immediately
- If poisoned: Seek medical help immediately
- **Emergency:** National Poison Control Center

## Tips for Success

### Buy Quality Inputs
- ✓ Use only KEPHIS-certified seeds
- ✓ Buy from authorized agro-dealers
- ✓ Check expiry dates
- ✓ Buy fertilizers in original bags

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use farm manure instead of fertilizers?**
A: Yes! Manure is great for soil health. Use 10-15 tons/ha. Best results when combined with a little fertilizer. Saves 30-40%.

**Q: What if I can't afford all recommended inputs?**
A: Get budget-adjusted recommendations showing what's essential vs. optional. Focus on seeds + base fertilizer first. Pests are optional until you see them.

**Q: When should I buy inputs?**
A: Buy before the season starts. Prices are better before rains. If you buy after rains start, prices increase.

**Q: Can I save seeds to replant?**
A: Yes, for open-pollinated varieties only. NOT for hybrid seeds (they won't breed true). Ask at input shop if variety is hybrid.

**Q: How do I know if my soil needs improvement?**
A: Get soil tested at agricultural office. Or follow the recommendations - if soil is poor, Fahamu Shamba will suggest amendments.

**Q: What's the difference between essential, important, and optional inputs?**
A: Essential = needed for good yield. Important = recommended if possible. Optional = nice to have if budget allows.

**Q: Can I reduce chemical use?**
A: Yes! Try organic methods: neem oil, soap spray, companion planting. Scout regularly and treat only when needed.
